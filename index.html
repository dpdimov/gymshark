Balance Sheet Detail
Gymshark is largely equity-funded through retained earnings. Debt was modest (£5M peak in 2019) and fully repaid by 2021. The balance sheet expanded from £3M to £189M, driven by inventory and receivables growth, with a notable cash drawdown in 2021 (£70M to £43M) as working capital ballooned.

DuPont Detail
ROA peaked at 90% in 2017 — driven by a 20% EBIT margin on 4.5x asset turnover. By 2021, ROA was 57%: lower margins (14%) offset by steady ~4x turnover. Leverage has been negligible — Gymshark has operated almost entirely on equity.

Cash Flow Statement
Gymshark ran a negative cash cycle for most years — payable days exceeded receivables + inventory days, meaning suppliers effectively financed operations. This inverted in 2021 when receivables surged to 49 days (from 11) and payables tightened, producing a £27M cash outflow despite record profits.
